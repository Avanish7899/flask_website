About Us

The National Council for Science & Technology Communication (NCSTC), under the Department of Science & Technology (DST), Government of India, collaborates with the National Institute of Technology Karnataka (NITK) to conduct a children-centric outreach program titled “Creating Awareness in Artificial Intelligence.” This initiative introduces secondary school children in Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu to the fundamentals of AI through interactive sessions, real-world applications like object detection and sentiment analysis, and hands-on experiments. The program aims to make learning AI fun and engaging while preparing students for the future.

Our Vision

"Our vision is to create a future where young minds are empowered with the knowledge and skills to thrive in a technology-driven world. We aim to demystify Artificial Intelligence (AI) and make it accessible to students, fostering curiosity, creativity, and innovation in the field of AI."

Meet the Team

Dr. Anand Kumar M

Principal Investigator

Associate Professor, Department of Information Technology, National Institute of Technology Karnataka, SCaLAR Lab

Dr. M. Anand Kumar’s research focuses on Natural Language Processing (NLP), Machine Translation for Indian languages, and Deep Learning for Text Analytics. He has published over 185 Scopus-indexed papers and received around 2200 citations on Google Scholar. He has led significant projects, including the "Computing Tools for Tamil Language Learning" funded by the Tamil Nadu government, the "Tamil-Malayalam Subtitle Translation System" for Sharp Software, and the "Q&A Extraction from Unstructured Documents" with EduMinster (U.S.). He is also a SERB-CRG awardee (2023-2026) for a project on "Deep Explainable Framework for Legal Text Retrieval and Summarization."

Our Partners and Supporters

Centre for System Design (CSD)

The Centre for System Design (A Centre of Excellence at NITK Surathkal) envisages an interdisciplinary approach and means for realization of successful engineering systems. Key components include system modeling & simulation, understanding system dynamics, system optimization, virtual and physical experimentation. The Centre facilitates an environment for solving modern engineering problems that transcend traditional boundaries, ensuring integration to meet overall design objectives.

Department of Science & Technology (DST)

The Department of Science & Technology (DST) was established in May 1971 with the objective of promoting new areas of science and technology. As a nodal department of the Government of India, DST is responsible for organizing, coordinating, and promoting S&T activities across the country. DST has sponsored this project to ensure the dissemination of AI knowledge and the development of scientific temperament among students.
